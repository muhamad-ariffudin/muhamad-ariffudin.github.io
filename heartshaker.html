Heart Shaker

spreads gratitude for a better world
My Role
UI/UX Designer, Web Developer
Duration
4 Month
Tools
Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Miro, Trello, Visual Studio, MySQL
Overview
Kento Kanemoto, the representative director of HEART SHAKER, created a Q&A community where people help each other. They established the world's first community of its kind and found that expressing gratitude motivates people to assist others. To spread gratitude, they formed the HEART SHAKER association, aiming to enlighten appreciative individuals and promote a grateful world. Their focus is on discovering, recognizing, and sharing gratitude-inspiring individuals, companies, and organizations.
CHALLENGE
Cultivating Emotional Engagement: The challenge of effectively engaging users emotionally through campaign updates to create a deeper connection with supported causes. Promoting Sustained Support: The challenge of encouraging users to make ongoing contributions, either to the same or related campaigns, in order to nurture a continuous cycle of support and gratitude. Establishing a Culture of Thankfulness: The challenge is to cultivate a culture of gratitude within the HEART SHAKER Q&A community by introducing a system of gratitude tokens (TKU), aimed at recognizing and sharing sources of inspiration among community members and beyond.
Design Process
Empathize
To better comprehend user concerns, I embarked on a research journey that began with an in-depth review of academic literature related to comparable applications. Furthermore, I carefully analyzed user feedback on the Play Store and closely examined recorded Ask Me Anything (AMA) sessions featuring current Hear Shaker users. This extensive investigation revealed several identified issues, such as insufficient progress information, limited access to campaign post, the incapacity to select preferred campaign updates, a lack of transparency concerning contribution and gratitude tokens (TKU), and the absence of rewarding features, among other challenges.
Define
In the next phase, I contemplated possible solutions to address these user problems using "How Might We" statements.
Ideate
Solution Idea
Based on user feedback, I've formulated the following "How might we" statements: How can I enhance campaign appeal through detailed progress, enable access to campaign post, provide user choice in followed campaigns, ensure transparent contribution, and implement a motivating reward system to motivate and engage users.
Next, I prioritized solutions based on the results of voting, with at least four main solutions: - Simplify the contributor's disbursement of contributions - Enhance contributor engagement to heighten their interest. - Implement rewards for contributors and establish a reward leveling system. - Integrate a bookmarking feature for users to monitor specific campaign posts. However, I didn't discard other solutions but instead grouped them into an affinity diagram to complement the application's performance, encompassing social features, wishlists, reward programs, and future features.
When creating the UI design, I need to consider resources and time constraints, so I prioritize features based on Heart Shaker's main challenge, which is revamping the campaign feature.
Our focus is on features that serve the following functions: - Create a section to prompt people to demonstrate appreciation by giving gratitude tokens (TKU). - Create campaign updates resembling photo and video stories, taking inspiration from Instagram. - Create compelling campaigns and updates while introducing a campaign bookmark feature. - Create a feature for sending personalized video/photo thank-you messages to other users.

Testing
I also conducted user testing to confirm pain points after redesigning the Heart Shaker application's user interface. Using the depth interview method, I asked several questions related to whether there were any obstacles or confusion with the design I had created. Here are the results of user interviews for each task we assigned: Task 1 (Login): There are no issues to report, and the information provided is straightforward and easy to understand. Task 2 (Contribution): There are no issues to report, and the information provided is straightforward and easy to understand. Task 3 (Photo/Video Campaign and Story): Quite interested in the new feature because it makes it easier for users who don't have much time to read. It's simpler. Viewing stories also minimizes user time usage. Information about disbursement and allocation is clear enough. Task 4 (Bookmark): No issues, but slightly confusing because there is no next step. Information is somewhat lacking because users cannot see whether there is a difference in the bookmarked information or not. Task 5 (sending personalized video/photo thank-you messages): No issues, there have been some updates so users can see detailed information; this innovation is helpful for users. Conclusion & Lesson Learned
The design created excels in the story and contribution history features but has some shortcomings in the bookmark feature. Improvements are needed based on the feedback and suggestions provided by users. Hopefully, this article can provide insights for readers and UI/UX designers looking to revamp similar applications.
